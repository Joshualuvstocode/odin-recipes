How To Make Jamiacan Rice + Peas!!



Description

Jamaican rice and peas — seasoned with the special trinity of thyme, scallion, and garlic — is a staple dish of the island. Like clockwork, my mom cooked Jamaican rice and peas with a baked chicken every Sunday. Its woodsy flavor is heightened by the addition of whole pimento seeds (aka allspice berries) and its subtle heat comes from the Scotch bonnet pepper.


ingredients

    13 cups water
    1/4 cup of milk (whole or half and half is best) 1 small onion, finely chopped 2 scallions, finely chopped
    3 garlic cloves, minced
    5 allspice berries
    5 thyme sprigs
    1 Scotch bonnet pepper
    1/2 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
    2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
    1/2 teaspoon black pepper
    1 (13.5-ounce) can unsweetened coconut milk
    2 cups long-grain white rice

steps

    Step 1: Gather your ingredients
    Step 2: In a large saucepan, cover the beans with the water and bring to a boil over moderately high heat. Stir in the onion, scallions, garlic, allspice, thyme, Scotch bonnet, ginger, 2 teaspoons salt, and 1/2 teaspoon pepper.
    Step 3: Stir in the coconut milk and bring to a simmer.
    Step 4: Cover and simmer over low heat until the beans are tender, about 1 hour; adjust the heat as necessary to maintain a gentle simmer.
    Step 5: Stir in the rice; cover and simmer over low heat until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed, about 30 minutes.
    Step 6: Remove from the heat and let steam for 10 minutes, then discard the thyme stems, allspice berries, and Scotch bonnet. Using a fork, fluff the rice and beans and season with salt.
    Step 7: Serve hot.
